a) Illustrate the characteristics of the statistical model for dealing with the Dugongs data.

  For this specific problem we have a data with two variables Y and X, where Y is the length and X is the age. For this specific problem we have to model different types of distributions for conditionated parameters of interests in respect to one main distribution of the data Y, which is a normal distribution.
  Looking for a Bayesian perspective, we don't have a conjugate analysis here. What we have is a likelihood in respect to the data Y and different priors for different parameters of interest, so we don't have a close formula to derive the posterior distribution and re-write our parameters of interests. So, in this situation we will use the likelihood and the priors to derive marginal posteriors for the parameters of interest.
  The proceedure used to derive the marginal posterior is a Markov Chain Monte Carlo algorithm, the Gibbs Sampling, which is a recursive algorithm with markov conditions and, by the property of the Law of Large Numbers, will approximate the value of the parameter of interest for the marginal posterior.

like<- function(y,mu,tau){
  n<-length(y)
  var<-1
  for(i in 1:n){
    var<-var*dnorm(y[i],mean = mu[i],sd=sqrt(tau))
  }
  return(var)
}
